Rounders Guide

Master Rounders

Your complete interactive guide to the classic British bat-and-ball game

Game Rules

Before You Start - Agree On:

  • 1. Innings format: Choose one: All batters bat once / 27 good balls / 3 outs / Whole team out
  • 2. Good balls per batter: Usually 1, but can be 2-3 if feeling generous (applies to everyone)

Teams & Players

  • • Two teams of 6-15 players ideally
  • • Maximum 9 players on field at once
  • • One team bats, one fields
  • • Teams switch after each innings
  • • Usually played over 2 innings

Fielding Positions

  • • One bowler - bowls to batters
  • • One backstop - behind batter
  • • Rest of team - on field or covering bases

Scoring

  • • 1 Rounder: Hit ball & reach 4th post
  • • ½ Rounder: Hit ball & reach 2nd or 3rd post (stop there!)
  • • ½ Rounder: Don't hit ball but reach 4th post
  • • ½ Rounder: Bowler bowls 2 consecutive no-balls
  • • ½ Rounder: Fielder obstructs batter
  • • ⚠️ Scores only count when you reach home safely!

Running Rules

  • • If you hit the ball - you MUST run (even on no-ball)
  • • If you don't hit on no-ball - you don't have to run
  • • If you don't hit good ball - you MUST run
  • • Keep contact with post (hand, bat, or foot)
  • • Can run when ball leaves bowler's hand
  • • If bowler has ball in square, can't leave post (but can finish run to next post)

Batting Rules

  • • Can bat one-handed or two-handed
  • • Wait in batting order away from 4th post
  • • Must run outside the batting square
  • • Can hit backwards and still score
  • • If hit backwards, wait at 1st post until ball leaves backward area

Bowling Rules

  • • Must bowl underarm with smooth action
  • • Ball must reach batter between knee & head height
  • • Ball must not bounce before reaching batter
  • • Ball must not be wide or straight at body
  • • Bowler's foot must stay in square during bowl
  • • Two consecutive no-balls = ½ rounder to batting team

Ways to Get Out

  • • Ball caught before bouncing
  • • Post you're running to is stumped
  • • Lose contact with post when bowler has ball in square
  • • Run inside batting square
  • • Overtake another runner (you run them out!)
  • • Two batters at same post (first one there is out)
  • • ⚠️ If out, lose any points from that run!

Winning the Game

  • • Team with most rounders after 2 innings wins
  • • Innings ends when agreed format is met
  • • If all batters out with no one left to bat, innings ends

Multiple Runners

  • • Can stay at posts between batters
  • • Can run when next ball leaves bowler's hand
  • • Can get out while waiting between posts
  • • Continue until you reach home or get out

Detailed Playing Rules

The Pitch

The pitch has four posts acting as bases arranged in a diamond. After batting, your aim is to run around all four posts to score. The batting square is where you stand to hit the ball. The backward area is the zone behind the batting square.

How to Score

1 Rounder: Hit the ball and reach 4th post (home) before the next ball is bowled.

½ Rounder: Hit the ball and reach 2nd or 3rd post (stop there and wait). Don't continue running or you might get out and lose the ½ rounder!

½ Rounder: Miss the ball but still reach 4th post.

Important: Your score only counts when you reach home safely and join the batting queue. If you get out during your run, you lose all points from that turn.

When Must You Run?

If you hit the ball: You MUST run, no matter what (even if it's a no-ball).

If you miss a good ball: You MUST run.

If you miss a no-ball: You DON'T have to run (but you can if you want, and you can still score).

No-Balls

A no-ball is called when:

  • Ball is above head or below knee height
  • Ball bounces before reaching batter
  • Ball is too wide or aimed at body
  • Bowler's bowling action isn't smooth underarm
  • Bowler's foot is outside the square during bowling

Penalty: Two consecutive no-balls to the same batter = ½ rounder for batting team.

Batter's options: Can choose to run on a no-ball and score normally, or not run. If you hit the ball, you must run regardless.

Backwards Area Rules

Batters can still score if they hit the ball backwards (behind the batting square). However, they must wait at 1st post until the ball is thrown back into the forward playing area. Once the ball leaves the backward area, they can continue running and score as normal.

Post Contact Rules

When waiting at a post, you must maintain contact with your hand, bat, or foot. If you're not touching the post, the fielding team can stump the next post to get you out. When the bowler has the ball in the bowling square, you cannot leave your post (but if you're already running between posts, you can complete your run to the next post).

Multiple Runners & Overtaking

Batters from previous turns can wait at posts and continue running when the next ball is bowled (or when the ball leaves the bowler's hand).

Important rule: You cannot have two batters at the same post. If two batters end up at the same post, the first one there is out.

Overtaking: If you overtake another runner from your team, the person you overtook is out (you've run them out!).

Obstruction

If a fielder obstructs a batter (blocks their path when they have right of way on the track), the batting team is awarded ½ rounder as a penalty.

End of Innings

An innings ends based on the format you agreed before the match:

  • All batters bat once (shortest game)
  • 27 good balls bowled
  • 3 outs recorded
  • Whole team out (longest game) - when all batters are out and no one is left to bat

Quick Decision Guide

✓ You're Safe / Allowed

  • ✓ Bowl underarm with smooth action
  • ✓ Hit ball backwards (wait at 1st)
  • ✓ Run on no-ball and score
  • ✓ Wait at post between batters
  • ✓ Use foot to touch post
  • ✓ Stop at 2nd/3rd for ½ rounder
  • ✓ Miss ball but reach 4th = ½ rounder

⚠ Must Know Rules

  • ⚠ Hit ball = MUST run (even no-ball)
  • ⚠ Miss good ball = MUST run
  • ⚠ Miss no-ball = DON'T have to run
  • ⚠ Score only counts when home safe
  • ⚠ 2 no-balls in row = ½ rounder
  • ⚠ Fielder obstructs = ½ rounder
  • ⚠ Can't leave post when bowler has ball

✗ You're Out!

  • ✗ Ball caught before bounce
  • ✗ Post stumped ahead of you
  • ✗ Not touching post (bowler has ball)
  • ✗ Run inside batting square
  • ✗ Overtake teammate
  • ✗ Two at same post (1st there is out)
  • ✗ If out, lose that turn's points!

Scoring Examples

✓ Score 1 Rounder

You hit the ball and run all the way to 4th post (home) before the next ball is bowled. You score 1 rounder!

✓ Score ½ Rounder (Option 1)

You hit the ball and reach 2nd post. You STOP there. You score ½ rounder. Next batter comes up, you run when ball leaves bowler's hand.

✓ Score ½ Rounder (Option 2)

You miss the ball but manage to run all the way to 4th post without getting out. You score ½ rounder!

✗ Score Nothing

You hit the ball and reach 2nd post (½ rounder). But you try to continue to 3rd and get stumped out. You lose all points from that turn!

Or, you only make it to 1st post, and then manage to get back home without being stumped out. You score nothing!
